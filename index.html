üî¨ STREAM Info ‚Äî In-depth

Science

Green chemistry focuses on designing chemical products and processes that reduce or eliminate hazardous substances. Key ideas include atom economy, safer solvents, and designing for degradation.

Technology

Tools like sensors, simulations, and data visualization (this app uses Chart.js) help scientists measure emissions, model reactions, and optimize processes before physical experiments.

Reading & Research

Effective research means evaluating primary literature, reviewing reliable government and academic sources, and comparing experimental methods and their environmental impact. Record sources and cite them in your project handout.

Engineering

Engineers design reactors and processes that maximize yield and minimize energy use. Examples: continuous-flow reactors, heat recovery systems, and reactor scaling methods that reduce waste.

Art & Communication

Visual design and storytelling make science accessible. Use infographics, clear color schemes, and animations (like the title) to explain complex ideas to judges and peers.

Math & Data

Quantitative skills are essential: calculating yields, carbon footprints, percent error, uncertainty, and visualizing results. Charts and trend lines turn numbers into persuasive evidence.

Ethics & Impact

Consider the social and environmental implications of chemical choices. Safer alternatives can improve public health, reduce cleanup costs, and support sustainable economies.

Careers & Skills

Fields that use green chemistry: sustainable materials, environmental engineering, renewable energy, regulatory science, and sustainable product design. Skills: data analysis, lab safety, coding for data tools, and science communication.

Project Ideas
  • Compare carbon footprints of two synthesis routes using the calculator and chart.
  • Design a poster linking atom economy to waste reduction with visuals.
  • Prototype a small "green" solvent substitution and measure differences.
